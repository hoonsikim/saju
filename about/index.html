What is Saju?

Saju (사주, 四柱) — literally "Four Pillars" — is the Korean tradition of reading a person's birth chart through the Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches of their year, month, day, and hour of birth. It shares roots with Chinese BaZi (八字) but carries its own interpretive conventions, refined in Korea for centuries.

This site gives you the real thing: the actual pillars, your Day Master, the Five Elements balance, the Ten Gods, hidden stems, twelve life stages, void branches, and ten-year luck cycles — not a Western horoscope dressed up in Eastern words.

How it works

The deterministic chart math can run in your browser, but every AI narrative — including the current free reading and any paid or custom reading — sends your birth date, local birth time, language, selected focus and applicable traditional gender setting to Cloudflare Workers and Anthropic's Claude API together with derived chart fields. City/country may be used by the free or Etsy flow when supplied; it is deliberately excluded from the direct Gumroad checkout. The free result keeps birth date/time and focus in the current page address so it can be reopened; reset removes them. Direct checkout sends the minimum current fulfillment fields — birth date/time, gender, language and focus — in the Gumroad URL, while Gumroad supplies the buyer email. Saju does not keep a second paid-checkout copy in browser storage. Direct delivery routes order/reading data through Gumroad, Cloudflare Workers, Anthropic and Resend; Etsy retains its own order and message records. We do not sell birth data or intentionally put it in analytics, advertising audiences, mailing lists or reusable training datasets. For Etsy made-to-order fulfillment, our 30-day deletion utility covers only the local raw-birth-data working directory, subject to a documented hold; it does not delete provider, transaction, email or message records. Anthropic states that standard API inputs and outputs are deleted within 30 days, with stated exceptions, and are not used for model training by default. Other providers apply their own terms.

Methodology

The chart computation is deterministic and reproducible, based on the open-source lunar-javascript library (v1.7.7), which encodes traditional Korean Saju conventions:

  • Year pillar (년주): boundary at 立春 (Start of Spring, ~Feb 4), not the lunar new year
  • Month pillar (월주): boundary at each 節 (節入) of the 24 solar terms
  • Day pillar (일주): 60-갑자 cycle, 子時 (23:00–01:00) treated as the start of the next day (夜子時 = 翌日)
  • Hour pillar (시주): 24h → 12지 mapping
  • Hidden stems (지장간), 12 stages (12운성), void branches (공망), major luck cycles (대운), shen sha (신살): 천을귀인·도화·역마): all computed via the lunar-javascript EightChar interface

True solar time (真太陽時) longitude correction is not currently applied — the birth city field is used as reading context only. For minute-level precision (hour-pillar boundary cases), please consult a credentialed practitioner.

Sources & References

This site does not claim to replace a credentialed practitioner; it is a software interpretation aimed at making the framework accessible globally.
